## Additional items worth flagging

### 1) Facility closure + state investigation remains an operator risk pattern
A public search result surfaced: **"Texas Health and Human Services releases investigation after facility closure"** from **Hays Free Press** (dated **2026-01-28**).

**Why it matters:**
- Even when a closure is local rather than statewide, the pattern matters for LTC operators and pharmacies: closures create messy transitions, med-cart reconciliation risk, controlled-substance accountability questions, transfer-order confusion, and family/referral fallout.
- For pharmacies, this is a reminder to keep a clean **closure / transfer playbook** ready: discharge destruction, emergency fills during resident moves, MAR handoff integrity, and documentation of who took custody of meds.

**Suggested watch action:**
- Keep a standing alert for **Texas closures, receiverships, abrupt ownership exits, or state investigations** involving ALFs / SNFs, especially in growth corridors between Austin, San Antonio, Houston, and DFW.

### 2) Sagora AI-monitoring test is a live signal, not just background trend
A same-day search result showed **McKnight’s Senior Living** reporting on **2026-03-10** that **Sagora Senior Living** and **Teton** are testing **AI-powered monitoring technology** at two properties.

**Why it matters for operators:**

**Why it matters for LTC pharmacies:**
- If operators get more real-time resident-risk visibility, they may expect faster turnaround on PRN availability, urgent delivery, refill exceptions, behavioral-med reviews, and med-change communication.
- Pharmacies that can pair dispensing reliability with clinical responsiveness will look stronger than vendors offering only basic fulfillment.

**Operational note:**
