# SHERIDAN_MISSOURI_LTC_APP_COPY

## Purpose
Concise, demo-ready in-app copy for a Missouri LTC pharmacy operations prototype. Tone is operator-facing: calm, direct, and action-oriented.

---

## Global UI Microcopy

### App positioning
**App title:** Missouri LTC Command Center  
**Primary subtitle:** See today’s risk, protect service, and move the queue.  
**Secondary subtitle:** Built for long-term care pharmacy operations across admissions, fills, deliveries, and after-hours exceptions.

### Primary actions
- **Review queue**
- **Assign owner**
- **Resolve blocker**
- **Message facility**
- **Escalate now**
- **Mark complete**
- **Open facility**
- **View route**
- **Log issue**
- **Add note**

### Status labels
- On track
- At risk
- Needs review
- Waiting on facility
- Waiting on prescriber
- Ready to fill
- Ready to deliver
- After-hours risk
- Escalated
- Closed

### Empty states
- **No open issues.** Today’s queue is clear right now.
- **Nothing waiting on pharmacy.** Any remaining work is external.
- **No late deliveries.** Route timing is currently on track.
- **No new ADT events.** Admissions, discharges, and transfers are caught up.
- **No emergency kit exceptions.** Restock and documentation look clean.

### Helper text
- Focus on items that can still affect today’s service.
- Aging work rises to the top automatically.
- Use notes to capture the next step, not the full backstory.
- When in doubt, assign an owner before moving on.

## 2) Facility Risk
**Module title:** Facility Risk  
**One-line description:** Which buildings create the most preventable noise, rework, or service exposure.  
**Expanded description:** Live scorecards that combine volume, operational friction, after-hours usage, communication reliability, and recurring exception patterns.

### Risk band copy
- **Stable:** Predictable workflow and low preventable noise.
- **Watchlist:** Service is intact, but repeat friction is building.
- **Unstable:** Rework, callbacks, or after-hours use are becoming routine.
- **Critical:** Immediate intervention needed to protect service or account health.

### Score explanation
**What this score means:** Higher risk does not always mean higher census. It means the account is consuming disproportionate time, creating avoidable exceptions, or showing signs of instability.

### Empty-state note
**No facilities are trending critical today.** Watchlist accounts still deserve review.

## Alert Banner Copy

### High priority
- **Route cutoff risk:** Unresolved fill issues may miss today’s delivery window.
- **ADT aging:** Admission turnaround is slipping at one or more facilities.
- **After-hours pattern:** Preventable STAT usage is trending up this week.
- **Facility instability:** Callback volume and unresolved exceptions are elevated.
- **Missing ownership:** Critical work is open with no assigned next step.

### Medium priority
- **Watchlist account:** Repeat friction is building at this facility.
- **Cycle fill drift:** Held items are rising ahead of schedule.
- **Restock lag:** Emergency kit documentation is incomplete.
- **Delivery variance:** One route is off normal timing.

### Positive signals
- **Queue recovered:** Critical items are back within service expectations.
- **Facility stabilized:** Exception volume has improved over the last 7 days.
- **Cycle fill protected:** High-risk holds were cleared before cutoff.
- **After-hours reduced:** Preventable STAT demand is trending down.

## Tooltip / Info Copy
- **Facility risk score:** Composite signal based on preventable noise, callback volume, after-hours usage, unresolved exceptions, and recent trend.
- **ADT aging:** Time since the resident move entered the queue without full resolution.
- **Route cutoff risk:** Likelihood an unresolved item will miss the planned delivery window.
- **Preventable STAT:** Urgent request that appears linked to workflow, stock, timing, or communication breakdown rather than unavoidable clinical need.
- **Held fill:** Prescription work that cannot move forward until a blocker is cleared.
